Where we diverge

The one to circle

Quality Time — his lowest, her second

This is the single widest gap in the data, and the one most likely to quietly hurt. It's Jordan's lowest channel (13%) and Laura's second-highest (20%) — which means the thing that most says "I love you" to Laura is the thing Jordan is least wired to offer, and least likely to remember. Book it like a meeting. It will not happen on its own.

Two Helpers

The shared wiring — and its shadow

The Enneagram is where our similarity goes deepest, and it comes with a warning label that's worth taking seriously.

2w3
The Helper, with an achiever wing — both of us, same type, same wing.
How it shows up in Jordan

The 2's need to be appreciated runs straight through his profile — Words of Affirmation, Woo, and the recognition-or-bitterness pattern. He gives by championing, connecting, and building things for people, and needs the appreciation spoken back.

How it shows up in Laura

The same 2 expresses through Acts of Service and Developer — she gives by doing, anticipating needs, and growing the people around her. The 3 wing adds drive and quiet ambition. She shows worth through what she does for others.

The shared shadow

Type 2's core trap is giving to get love, then not asking for what you need — and quietly resenting it when the giving isn't matched. Two 2w3s can fall into a loop where both over-give, both stay tuned to the other's needs, and neither voices their own. Add Jordan's recognition wound and Laura's accommodate-under-pressure tendency, and you get two people working hard to care for each other while both feel subtly unseen.

The remedy

State your needs out loud, as directly as you state each other's. For a pair of Helpers, naming "here's what I need from you" is the growth edge — it's harder than giving. Jordan, specifically ask Laura what she needs rather than assuming; Laura, let Jordan carry you sometimes instead of always being the one who does. The health of two 2s is a relationship where both are allowed to receive.

The short version

We want the same life and move at the same speed — two Helpers pointed at the same horizon. Where we differ, we mostly complete each other: his voice and her depth, his energy in bursts and her steady engine. The work is small and specific — he protects the quiet time and does the doing; she says it out loud and reaches first; and both of us learn to ask, not just give.
