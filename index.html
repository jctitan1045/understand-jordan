Where the frameworks disagree

The one place the sources fight — and what to do about it

Human Design says

Wait for invitation. Don't initiate — wait to be recognized and asked.

Every other assessment says

The opposite. Influence 74, Woo, Extraverted 78% — a man who initiates constantly. Track record: founded a run club, launched an agency, organized meetups, pitched partnerships.

The honest synthesis: ignore "wait for invitation" as literal advice — it contradicts how I demonstrably succeed. But keep the underlying insight, because it rhymes with the recognition pattern: my initiations land best where I'm already recognized and wanted, and worst where I'm selling myself cold to an audience that hasn't asked. My speaking career runs on invitations. My best clients come through referrals, not cold outreach. That is the usable version of the Projector claim.

The field guide

Working with me & loving me

The same person shows up in both contexts — here's the operating manual for each.

Verbal affirmation is my primary channel — by a wide margin.

Words of Affirmation 33%; Quality Time, my lowest, 13%. The Success/Bitterness theme doubles down: unrecognized effort curdles into resentment faster in me than in most.

Example: If I plan a weekend trip and organize every detail, "you thought of everything, this is amazing" lands harder than the trip itself. Silence — even contented silence — reads as the trip not mattering. One specific verbal appreciation per day is a reasonable floor.

I show love by organizing, improving, and championing.

ENFJ + Maximizer + Arranger: I'll notice your goals, believe in them loudly, and start building infrastructure around them.

Example: Mention once you want to get fitter and I may show up with a training plan, a gym rec, and two friends who want to join. This is affection, not criticism. If it feels like pressure, say "I love that you want to help; right now I just need you to listen," and I'll adjust. I respond to direct instruction far better than to withdrawal.

My energy is a budget, not a well.

The Projector frame and Steadiness 14 agree: I burn bright in bursts and deplete. I fill calendars past what my energy supports, then crash.

Example: A week with a client launch, a run club event, a men's circle, volunteering, and three dinners ends with me flat on the couch — and I won't have seen it coming. A good partner names it before the crash ("your Thursday and Friday are both triple-booked — pick one"), not after.

I commit to direction, not routine.

Judging 68% wants a defined shared future — the plan, the timeline, the vision. Steadiness 14 means the weekly rhythm inside that plan keeps mutating.

Example: I'll happily commit to "we're building a life between Medellín and Mexico" and then change the next three months' logistics four times. Read the constancy at the level of destination, not calendar.

Criticism lands harder than I show.

Feeling 63%, Turbulent 53%, plus the Bitterness shadow. I absorb direct feedback well when it's warm and private; cold or public criticism gets replayed at 2 a.m.

Example: "The dinner ran long and I felt like an accessory," said over coffee the next morning, gets a real apology and changed behavior. The same sentiment as a cutting remark in front of friends gets a week of distance.

Freedom is a top-three value.

Monitoring, ultimatums, and control produce the opposite of the intended result. The 1/3 profile adds a wrinkle: I learn through my own trial and error, so I'll sometimes need to make the mistake even when warned.

How to relate to me

  • Affirm specifically, not generically. "You handled that awkward moment well" beats "you're great."
  • Bring your own ambitions and full life — I bond through mutual growth and get restless around passivity.
  • Guard unstructured time together, because I won't create it. Book it like a meeting if you have to.
  • Name overcommitment before the crash, not after.
  • Deliver hurt plainly, early, and in private. Ambiguity spirals me; clarity resolves me.
  • Let me take my swings. "I told you so" is the single most corrosive sentence available to you.

Profiles that fit

  • Grounded and steady — a baseline calm that offsets my volatility of pace without policing it.
  • Independent, with her own projects and friendships. Two full lives that overlap beat one orbiting mine.
  • Verbally generous. A partner who loves only through acts of service or quality time loves me in a dialect I barely speak.
  • A direct communicator. Hints and passive-aggression fail against someone moving this fast.
  • Caution: another low-steadiness, high-stimulation partner makes a thrilling relationship where nobody rests and nobody minds the store.

I front-load value.

Vision, pitch, network, launch. Woo, Positivity, and Strategic mean I open doors and spot angles.

Example: Give me a new venture and within two weeks I have the positioning, the first three partnership conversations, a financial model with scenario cases, and momentum. This is my highest-leverage zone — structure the partnership so I stay in it.

The middle 80% drifts unless someone else owns it.

Steadiness 14 plus the Projector energy pattern: recurring operations are where my ventures go to stall.

Example: I'll design a flawless ClickUp workflow for client delivery, run it energetically for six weeks, then my attention migrates to the next launch. Don't moralize this — staff it. Every venture needs a named operator who owns the boring recurring core, in writing, from day one.

Research first, then contact with reality.

The 1/3 Investigator/Martyr pattern shows in how I handle risk: I stress-test contracts and models before committing — I've caught structural flaws in loan agreements and rental management contracts before signing — and I still expect some ventures to fail informatively.

Assumption to adopt: I'll do the diligence, and I'll also want to run small real-world experiments rather than debate hypotheticals forever.

Fast, gut-informed decisions, revised without ego.

Splenic authority + D 58 + J 68%. I commit in the meeting and adjust when data arrives.

Example: Present me three options with numbers and I pick one before the call ends. Present a vague "let's think about directions" agenda and I get visibly impatient. If you need slow lanes (legal, diligence), negotiate them explicitly — "contracts get five business days" works; unexplained delay reads as weakness.

Convention carries zero weight; data carries all of it.

Tradition 30, Knowledge 34, Strategic in top five. Arguments from "industry standard" or hierarchy bounce off. Arguments from figures, leverage, and first principles land.

Example: "Everyone in this market charges retainers" moves me nowhere. "Retainer clients have 3× the lifetime value of project clients in our own books" changes my behavior that week.

Recognition is fuel and vulnerability at once.

Acknowledge my contributions specifically and I over-deliver. Ignore my wins or absorb my credit and I disengage, then embitter. The flip side: flattery lowers my guard. The partners worth having tell me hard truths warmly — anyone leading with how visionary I am should trigger my own contract-level scrutiny.

My initiations work best warm.

My hit rate through referrals, invitations, and existing community is dramatically higher than through cold outreach. Build business development around my recognition network — speaking, referral partners, community events — not around me grinding cold pipelines, which wastes my strongest asset and drains my limited energy on my weakest game.

How to relate to me

  • Lead with the conclusion; attach the data. I tune out preamble.
  • Put ownership in writing at formation: who owns ops, who owns growth, how decisions split, what exit looks like.
  • Give me the stage-facing work; keep me out of bookkeeping and recurring admin.
  • Challenge me hard, in private, with numbers. I distrust agreement that comes too easily.
  • Match my decision speed or pre-negotiate exceptions.
  • Work inside my systems (ClickUp, documented processes). Working around them loses information and trust.
  • Credit me specifically when I deliver, especially in front of others. It buys disproportionate commitment.
  • Watch my energy, not just my output. If I'm grinding maintenance work, something is misallocated — reassign it before quality slips.

Profiles that fit

  • The ideal complement: a high-steadiness, high-conscientiousness operator who finds recurring execution satisfying (Discipline, Consistency, Achiever, Responsibility). I supply deal flow and architecture; they run the machine.
  • A financial pessimist. My Positivity makes my default forecast sunny. I already build the scenarios — I need someone emotionally committed to the Bear case.
  • Low ego about the spotlight. I'll be the public face. A partner who needs equal billing resents me within a year.
  • Direct, thick-skinned, warm. I give candid feedback and need it back, wrapped in basic respect.
  • Caution: a second visionary influencer (two pilots, no crew), or a high-Tradition process guardian who argues from "the standard way."
The section I need most

Energy management

Every source agrees on the failure mode: I take on more than my energy sustains, because my enthusiasm writes checks my stamina can't cash.

2
Count my active fronts.

More than three simultaneous ventures in launch mode is the historical warning line.

Protect one recovery block per week that nothing colonizes.

4
Read bitterness as an alarm, not a character shift.

When sarcasm about obligations or complaints about being unappreciated appear, that's the depletion-plus-underrecognition alarm. The fix is usually subtraction of commitments plus explicit recognition — in that order.

The short version

Jordan is a momentum-builder and people-assembler who researches deeply, decides on instinct, learns by crashing into reality, and runs on recognition, freedom, and visible impact. Put him at the front of the stage and the start of the race. Pair him with people who love the work he avoids. Tell him the truth warmly, credit him specifically, guard his recovery time, and never try to control him — it's the one reliable way to lose him, in business or in love.
